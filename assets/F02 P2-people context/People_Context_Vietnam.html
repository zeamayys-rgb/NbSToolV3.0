Educationi

Within the nearest province of Lào Cai, 91.4% of people 15 years of age and above are literate; specifically 94.2% of males and 88.6% of females. The province distributes teaching staff across kindergarten, general education, vocational schools and universities, and provides education access to children, pupils, university and vocational students — with particular accommodation of women and ethnic-minority teachers and learners.

Economyi

The Province of Lào Cai gains revenue from various sources, with the top 3 main sectors being mining & minerals, agriculture & forestry, and tourism services. In 2024, the GDP at current prices for Lào Cai was VND 78,400 bn, with a GDP per capita of VND 100.4 M. The monthly income per capita at current prices was about VND 4.1 M. Economic indicators reveal a Gini ratio of 40.6% in 2024, while the CPI is 122.8. Regarding social insurance: 34% of the population was covered by social insurance, 93% had health insurance, and 28% were engaged in unemployment insurance.

Healthi

There are 196 health facilities in the Lào Cai area, including pharmacies, dentists, hospitals, midwives, doctors and clinics. The number of medical staff, doctors, midwives and nurses in this province is 4,120, 680, 540 and 1,860, respectively. The prevalence of malnutrition (height-for-age) among under-5 children was 24.8%. In 2024, the percentage of fully vaccinated infants reached 88.6%, an increase of 1.4 percentage points compared to 2023.

Housing & Human Settlementsi

In 2024, Lào Cai saw 97% of households using electricity from the national grid. The proportion of households in durable structures reached 58%, while 34% lived in semi-permanent houses and 8% in simple/permanent houses. The population accessing hygienic water sources was 89%, and 72% of the urban population was served by a centralized clean-water supply. In 2024, 184,000 tons of solid waste were collected, of which 142,000 tons were treated to national criteria; 81% of the population used toilets daily. Land use comprised 148k ha agricultural production, 372k ha forestry, 14k ha homestead, and 42k ha specially-used land.
