Singapore is a city-state — all figures are reported at the national level, which already represents complete local coverage.
Social (Demography) i

At the national level, Singapore has a population of 5.92 M people, with 1.43 M resident households and an average household size of 3.1 persons. The population density is 8,058 per sq km, with a growth rate of 1.6% per year, a gender ratio of 958 males per 1,000 females, and in 2024, 34,500 new immigrants (new citizens and PRs granted) were recorded.

Education i

At the national level, 4.21 M residents have completed schooling in 2024, and the literacy rate among residents aged 15 and above is 97.6%. The national educational infrastructure includes schools across all levels, served by 33,800 teachers, with 462,000 students enrolled as of 2024.

Economy i

At the national level, the median monthly household income in Singapore is SGD 11,300 as of 2024. National GDP at current prices was SGD 715 B, with a GDP per capita of SGD 120,800 and a growth rate of approximately 2.6%, while the Gini coefficient is 0.371. National land use comprises ~520 ha private agricultural land, ~680 ha non-private agricultural land, and the remainder across other categories.

Health i

In the selected planning area, there are 168 health facilities. At the national level the healthcare workforce comprises 58,400 medical workers, of whom 16,200 are doctors, as of 2024. Nationally, the prevalence of disability is 3.4%, the most common conditions are hypertension, diabetes and hyperlipidaemia, and the percentage of fully vaccinated infants reached 96.8% in 2024.

Housing & Human Settlements i

At the national level, the housing landscape in 2024 consisted of 1.07 M HDB flats, 290,000 condominiums and apartments, and 72,000 landed properties, occupied by 5.92 M residents. The three primary drinking water sources are local catchment, NEWater, and desalinated water; the water supply produced 1,990 million litres/day with consumption at 141 litres per capita/day, and 100% of households have access to piped water. Nationally, 4.2 M tonnes of solid waste were collected, and nature reserves cover 3,347 hectares.
