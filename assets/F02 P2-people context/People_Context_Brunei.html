Social (Demography)i

The selected area is located in Brunei-Muara District (daerah), which has a population of 308,000 people, a population density of 490 per sq km, a population growth rate of 0.9% per year, and a gender ratio of 106 males per 100 females. At the national level, Brunei has 78,400 households (average household size 5.4 persons), of which a portion are potentially impacted by the project. In 2024, the country recorded 14,200 immigrants and 9,600 emigrants, with a migration trend of +1.2% over 2020–2024.

Employmenti

The unemployment and underemployment rates in Brunei-Muara are 4.9% and 6.1% respectively, with an employment rate of 95.1% and a labour force participation rate of 65.8% as of 2024. At the national level, the majority of people are employed in the public administration, oil & gas, and wholesale & retail industries, with an employment trend of +0.8% over 2020–2024.

Educationi

At the national level, 312,000 people in Brunei have graduated from school and 58,000 from university. The national literacy rate is 97.6% (98.4% of males and 96.7% of females, a gender gap of 1.7 percentage points), and the national educational infrastructure includes schools by level served by 6,200 teachers, with 108,000 students enrolled as of 2024.

Economyi

Healthi

At the national level, Brunei has 54 health facilities, including 4 public and 2 private hospitals. The national healthcare workforce comprises 5,800 medical workers, of whom 820 are doctors, as of 2024. Nationally, the prevalence of stunting among children under 5 is 19.7%, while the percentage of fully vaccinated infants reached 93.8%.

Housing & Human Settlementsi

In 2024, the housing landscape in Brunei-Muara District consisted of 61% permanent, 31% semi-permanent, and 8% non-permanent dwellings. At the national level, 452,000 people occupy housing, with production and demand trending up. The national three primary drinking water sources are piped (treated), bottled / refill, and rainwater; 99% of households have access to safe water. The supply system produced 820 ML per day with consumption at 410 litres per capita/day. Nationally, 218,000 tonnes of solid waste were collected, 96% of the population used toilets daily, and permanent reserved forests cover 238,000 hectares.
