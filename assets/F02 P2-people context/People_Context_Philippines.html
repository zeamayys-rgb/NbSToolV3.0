Social (Demography)i

The selected area is located in Quezon Province, CALABARZON (Region IV-A), home to a population of 2.41 M people, encompassing 612,000 households, with 1.23 M males and 1.18 M females potentially impacted by the project. The population density is 147 per sq km, with an annual growth rate of 1.5%/year. The population pyramid falls into the expansive (young) category. The net migration rate stands at -0.4%, with notable out-migration to Metro Manila.

The unemployment and underemployment rates in Quezon Province are 4.3% and 12.1%, respectively, with a labour force participation rate of 64.2%. The majority of the population was employed in the agriculture sector, primarily working as skilled agricultural & fishery workers as of 2024.

Educationi

Within the CALABARZON region, 284,000 employed individuals hold college degrees, while 96,000 are employed with undergraduate qualifications. The majority of employed people graduated from senior high school as of 2024. The functional literacy rate stands at 93.8% for females and 91.2% for males. The region's educational infrastructure includes 418 public and 262 private senior high schools.

Economyi

In Quezon Province, the GDP growth rate during 2024 was approximately 5.8%. This growth was driven by the services, agriculture, and manufacturing sectors. In the broader CALABARZON region, GDP per capita was PhP 198,000 in 2024, with a regional Gini Ratio of 42.3% and CPI of 124.6. The poverty incidence among families in the province was estimated at 9.2%.

The prevalence of underweight children under 5 reached 16.4%, while stunting affected 21.7% of children.

Housing & Human Settlementsi

The three primary sources of water supply in CALABARZON are piped water (own use), protected well, and tube well / borehole, serving 368,000, 142,000, and 88,000 households respectively. Household wastewater is mostly managed through private flush toilets connected to septic tanks, with the remainder through shared and other facilities.
