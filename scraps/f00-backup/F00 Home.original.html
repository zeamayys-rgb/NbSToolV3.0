Definition of NbS

What are Nature-based Solutions?

Actions to protect, sustainably manage, and restore ecosystems — addressing societal challenges while delivering measurable benefits across three intertwined dimensions.

Climate

Carbon stocks, biomass and emissions avoided — quantified from vegetation cover for credible mitigation.

People

Socio-economic resilience and GEDSI indicators — keeping local communities at the centre of every project.

Nature

Biodiversity and ecosystem integrity — tracked through degradation layers and habitat condition over time.

The Criteria of High-Quality NbS

What makes an NbS project credible

Every project documented in the tool is measured against the ASEAN MRV framework for high-quality Nature-based Solutions.

1

Addresses societal challenges

Tied to a clear climate-adaptation, mitigation or resilience need for the community it serves.

2

Designed at an appropriate scale

Scoped spatially to the ecosystem and landscape, not arbitrary administrative boundaries.

3

Delivers net biodiversity gain

Improves habitat condition and ecosystem integrity, tracked through degradation layers.

4

Economically viable

Backed by a credible baseline that can attract and withstand large-scale climate finance.

5

Inclusive & equitable governance

Centres local organisations and communities, with GEDSI indicators built into monitoring.

6

Continuously monitored & verified

Carried into a long-term MRV plan so impact is measured, reported and independently verified.
